Crowded Rooms and Quiet Homes

  • I still remember the cold december night
  • How warm my friends house was and
  • How comforting was the lamps warm white light,
  • My friend was hosting a party and needed a hand
  • Trying to luminate the dull rental walls with festival light,
  • Soon enough the house was filled with faces and voices
  • Talking, Laughing, Gasping and making all sorts of noises.
  • I took a chair and sat with a jolly group
  • Introduced myself hoping to find someone new,
  • They greeted me with a smile and a lot of compassion
  • But they were already lost in stories that I barely knew,
  • Soon the voices turned into hum and my mind buzzed with confusion
  • The room was not warm anymore and the lights faced occlusion,
  • I asked myself, “When did this clique turn into a crowd?”
  • Today, I was not alone but lonely
  • I still remember the hot july morning,
  • How cold my own house was and
  • How energizing was the suns playful light,
  • I made myself a brunch listening to my favorite band
  • Trying to catch up with the tunes while getting the words right,
  • Soon I had nothing to do and was out of choices
  • Singing, Tapping, Clapping and making all sorts of noises.
  • I took a book and sat with my glasses on
  • Picked up where I left off hoping to reach the epilogue,
  • Poured myself a fresh cup of coffee and had some cookies on the side
  • Read a few pages before I was lost in stories those printed words hide
  • Soon, the cup was empty but my thoughts flowed like a riptide
  • The sun was bright, and my hope was amplified
  • I asked myself, “When did my house turn into a home?”
  • Today, I was alone but not lonely
